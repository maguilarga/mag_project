Summary: Latitude .vs. X

"What's the weather like as we approach the equator?" Anyone would answer: "Duh. It gets hotter...". But ... can we prove it

The purpose of our project is to analyze how weather changes as you get closer to the equator to demonstrate that the latitude affects the temperature and, possibly, other factors.

To perform the analysis, we used the OpenWeatherMap API to gather data on more than 500 cities; we gathered data on Temperature, Humidity, Cloudiness and Wind Speed.

Once we had the data, we used Matplotlib to plot the data and find a correlation between the different factors and the position of the city in the globe.

The conclusion? Latitude certainly affects the temperature of the place, as well as the humidity but has no discerning effect on cloudiness or wind speed.

Feel free to navigate the site to look at the original data gathered and the charts that drove the conclusions.
